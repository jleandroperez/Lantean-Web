This post is... pretty much, a note for my future self. I own a KitchenAid fridge (late 2006: <strong>KBLC36FMS</strong>), and thing is, it started constantly beeping.

Nope. It's not the door's sensor. It's not the water filter either. Odds are: the compressor is unable to start.

&nbsp;

<h3>Diagnose Mode</h3>

Push for 3 seconds both, the <strong>Reset Filter Percent</strong> and <strong>Power</strong>, and the fridge will get into diagnose mode.

There are two numeric displays in the fridge, and... this handy table will help you read the status of each one of the components.

<img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2350" src="/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Diagnostic-Chart.jpg" alt="Diagnostic Chart" width="300" height="216" />

You cycle thru steps by pressing the <strong>Reset Filter</strong> about 3 seconds. That's, pretty much, how you confirm it's the compressor!

&nbsp;

<h3>Fixing things up!</h3>

Thanks to this <a href="https://www.stevejenkins.com/blog/2014/03/how-to-fix-a-whirlpool-kitchenaid-w10219463-2307028-control-board-for-6/">super handy post</a>, i learnt that it's a bad relay, along with a failing capacitor (hey Steve, if you ever read this: <strong>thank you SO much!!!!</strong>).

For this fix, you'll need:

- [Omron Power Relay: G5Q-14-DC12](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00HPLLNSK/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_asin_title_o02__o00_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1)
- [Panasonic 220uF 63v capacitor](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00MMYT2GK/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_asin_title_o01__o00_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1)

Things are quite straightforward. You'll need to replace the second relay from the left, along with capacitor C32.

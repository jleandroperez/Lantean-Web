<p style="text-align: left;">I've gotta say this. I'm a big fan of J.J.Abrams director. He managed to develop a fresh sci-fy style, which looks really attractive. If you compare science fictions movies from the past 10 years, with the latest Star Trek: Into Darkness movie... you'd be impressed.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/star-trek-into-darkness1.png"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1093" title="Star Trek: Into Darkness" alt="Star Trek: Into Darkness" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/star-trek-into-darkness1.png" width="628" height="400" /></a></p>

What i like the most of the new movies is the way they've recreated the whole Trek technology. I'm not into hi tech visual effects... but the technology that's shown into the movie could deceit even guys with PhD's... the transporter looks super real!.

Luckily, although the movie is a couple months away, we got to check out the trailer... right now!!.  It will be released on May 17th, and even the <a href="http://www.startrekmovie.com/">website</a> is already up!.

By the way... J.J.Abrams is the father of Fringe. A sci-fi series that has been around for the past couple of years. If you have enough spare time i suggest you give it a shot.

And last but not least... if you're into SciFy technology, i recommend you read <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Physics-Impossible-Scientific-Exploration-Teleportation/dp/0307278824/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361020887&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=physics+of+the+impossible">Physics of the Impossible</a>, written by Professor Michio Kaku.

In such book, he got to analyze every single Trek technology, and he gives an educated estimate of when the technology could potentially become a reality. For instance... the Warp Drive is estimated to show up in the next 100 years.

I don't expect to live 100 years... but maybe, in my next incarnation, i'll be able to jump into the Enterprise... why not???.

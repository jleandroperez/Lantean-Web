<p>I started using Gmail around 2004, when it was invite only. I remember the UX being insanely fast (it was hard to believe it was <em>just</em> a web app!).</p>

<p>At some point in that decade, Google introduced Apps, free to everyone. It allowed you to use Gmail along with your custom domain. (Wow!)</p>

<p>At the time, I was a student, and loved everything Google. So immediately jumped in.</p>

<p>Years passed by, things happened...</p>

- [Google Buzz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Buzz) was integrated into everyone's gmail
- Since Buzz failed, [we were forced into Google Plus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google%2B)
- [Google Wave was killed](https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2010/08/wave-cancellation-google-gives-up-on-next-gen-messaging/)
- [Google Reader was also murdered](https://arstechnica.com/search/?ie=UTF-8&q=google+reader)
- Google Hangouts was also introduced, and integrated into Gmail
  - * Hangouts triggers my CPU's fan, instantaneously.
- [Google Inbox was sadly retired](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2019/04/here-lies-google-inbox-a-radical-rethink-of-how-email-should-work/)

<p></p>

<p>On January 2021, Google announced that the free Google Apps tier <a href="https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2022/01/google-tells-free-g-suite-users-pay-up-or-lose-your-account/">would no longer be free</a>, and users would be forced to either buy a subscription, or loose access.</p>

<p>Shortly after, the tone was lowered, and <a href="https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2022/01/google-relents-legacy-g-suite-users-will-be-able-to-migrate-to-free-accounts/">alternate options were announced</a>.</p>

<p><strong>I am thankful for having enjoyed at least a decade of service, for free. I sincerely am.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Maintaining a service has a real life cost: engineers / infrastructure. And I'm more than willing to pay for it.</strong></p>

<p>But not to Google.</p>

<p>I'm done with cluttered interfaces, and I'm done of being forced into products I don't want or need.</p>

<p>I'm just done with Google.</p>

<p>As of now, I've fully migrated <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT212514">my mail provider over to iCloud+ Mail</a> (which now supports custom domains), and I just regret not having done this switch, long, long ago.</p>
